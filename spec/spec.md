# Goals
Create a professional, calm, non-flashy website for **Brush Mountain Designs**, a web development agency that serves small local businesses.  
The website should look like a real client deliverable, demonstrating the quality, clarity, and restraint that clients can expect from their own websites.

The outcome should build trust, communicate competence, and clearly explain services without hype.

---

# Constraints
- The website must be **simple, professional, and not flashy**
- No animations, gradients, or trendy visual effects
- No agency buzzwords or sales-driven language
- Content must be written in plain, non-technical English
- Mobile-first and responsive
- Fast-loading, static output
- Semantic HTML and basic accessibility best practices required
- Do not introduce pages, sections, or features not explicitly defined
- Follow scope strictly and avoid feature creep

---

# Context
Brush Mountain Designs is a small web development agency focused on building websites for:
- Small local businesses
- Service providers
- Trades
- Medical and professional offices
- Nonprofits

The agency’s own website should **reflect the same style and quality as a finished client website**, not a flashy agency showcase.  
The audience is non-technical business owners who value reliability, clarity, and long-term support.

---

# Deliverables
The agent(s) should produce:
- A complete multi-page website structure
- Clear, professional page copy for each page
- Clean layout and section hierarchy
- SEO-friendly structure using semantic HTML
- Pages:
  - Home
  - About
  - Services
  - Contact
- Content that can be used directly or with minimal editing

---

# Out of Scope
- No portfolios or case studies
- No pricing tables
- No testimonials
- No animations or motion effects
- No blog or CMS
- No advanced interactivity
- No overly creative or experimental design choices

---

# Agents
- Router Agent
- Content Agent
- Structure Agent

---

# Responsibilities

## Router Agent
- Interpret the overall goal and constraints
- Route tasks to the appropriate agents
- Ensure scope and tone consistency across outputs

## Content Agent
- Write all page copy
- Maintain a calm, professional, plain-language tone
- Avoid marketing fluff and jargon
- Write for non-technical small business owners

## Structure Agent
- Define page structure and section hierarchy
- Ensure semantic HTML structure
- Enforce accessibility and SEO best practices
- Keep layouts simple, readable, and consistent

---

# Success Criteria
The final website should:
- Feel trustworthy and approachable
- Look like a real client-ready small business website
- Clearly explain what Brush Mountain Designs does
- Make a small business owner feel confident reaching out
